About the Masters of Horary

Our foundation rests on the shoulders of the great traditional astrologers who codified the art of horary astrology.

William Lilly (1602–1681)

One of the most famous English astrologers, William Lilly's masterpiece, Christian Astrology (1647), remains one of the most comprehensive and important texts in traditional horary astrology. His methodical approach to answering specific questions based on the exact time they are asked forms the core of our practice.

Guido Bonatti (13th Century)

An Italian mathematician, astronomer, and astrologer. His massive work, Liber Astronomiae, was a standard astrological text for centuries. Bonatti's meticulous rules and aphorisms are essential for determining the validity and outcome of horary charts.

Dorotheus of Sidon (1st Century)

A Hellenistic astrologer who wrote a highly influential instructional poem on astrology known as the Pentateuch. His work laid early foundations for electional and interrogational (horary) astrology that later Persian, Arabic, and European astrologers expanded upon.

Our Mission

We aim to bring these ancient, time-tested methods into the modern era through our blog, upcoming video series, free guides, and almanacs. By mastering these foundational techniques, we build true authority in the astrological community.
